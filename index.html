Our Story

From a small oven
to four beloved locations.

La Farine began in 1987 when Marie-Claude Dupont converted her grandmother's farmhouse kitchen into the city's first artisan bakery. With nothing but a wood-fired oven, a worn copy of The Art of French Pastry, and flour from the local mill, she baked her way into the neighbourhood's heart.

Today, her granddaughter Sophie leads the team — still using the original sourdough starter, still rising before dawn, still refusing shortcuts. Every loaf is shaped by hand. Every croissant is laminated with time and patience.

We source our flours from single-origin heritage grain farms, our eggs from free-range flocks within 50 miles, and our butter from a small creamery in Vermont. What you taste is the land, the craft, and the care — nothing more.

🌾 Heritage Grains

We use stone-milled heirloom wheat, rye, and spelt from small farms committed to regenerative agriculture.

⏳ Slow Fermentation

No shortcuts. Our doughs ferment for 18–36 hours, developing complex flavour and better digestibility.

🌍 Minimal Waste

Day-old bread becomes croutons and breadcrumbs. Fruit scraps go into jam. We aim for zero landfill.

🤝 Community

We donate unsold goods nightly to local shelters, and offer paid apprenticeships to young bakers each year.
